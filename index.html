Weaknesses

Weaknesses exposed by COVID-19
  • Not all congregational members are feeling equally cared for
  • Pledging is significantly negatively impacted by the financial downturn
Poor accessibility in sanctuary space
  • No wheelchair access to the circle other than top or bouncing down the stairs
  • Stair maneuvering for those who are more fragile ambulators
  • Sound accessibility in some locations in the Great Hall and Dining Room
Waters House is not accessible other than the first floor entrance through youth room and sort of through the library
Availability of services is limited (one service, Sunday morning, on location).
Our ambitions exceed our financial contributions and other sources of income
  • Our expectations have not taken changing financial capacities into account
  • Inability to let go of some (historic? legacy?) expenses as we add others which leads to increased expectations of staff
  • Lack of adequate data collection to help with some financial decision making
  • We are not fiscally balanced. A lot of people pledge a little bit, with a few responsible for pledging a lot.
Inadequate financial resources dedicated to physical campus
  • Difficult to respond to expensive emergencies
  • Several trees have fallen in the back yard or have needed to be taken down
  • Trellis Wall replacement
Less than optimal use of our physical campus
  • No office space for Music Dir
  • Interruptions in main office are regular
  • Distance of RE from Congregation
History of passive avoidance of conflict, difficult topics
Silos
  • Not all areas of the congregation rowing in the same direction
  • Improving in some places, not changing in others, and backsliding in some.
Lack cohesive approach as a congregation to how we are engaged in community
  • Inadequate communication/sharing of committee and team activities
Weak relationships between the youth/young adults and the older congregational leadership
Weak relationships with the wider community
ministerial fits-and-starts
  • Lack of consistency and specific protocols for ministry
No cohesive commitment to environmental sustainability and climate change
  • Issues with the physical building that might be able to be addressed but that are not.
  • Lack of attention to green practices.
  • No culture of car-pooling.
No leadership development process
Membership does not reflect demographics of the capital region
  • Racial
  • LGBTQ
  • Socio-economic
  • Age
  • Ability
  • Ethnic
Care for each other
  • Rides (This has improved but there is still room to grow)
  • Connection outside of church, feeling cared for
Governance shift room for improvement:
  • Communication of expectations between board and its committees
  • Understanding in wider congregation about the governance structure
Not always listening well to each other
  • We tend to listen to respond rather than to understand
Conflict around our social justice impact and efforts: messaging, expectations and perceptions
  • Connecting individual efforts to congregation-wide action
  • Basing expectations on stories of historical actions
  • Learning and deciding to row together
  • Taking into account white supremacy culture
  • Requests for change are met with resistance
  • A self-narrative of social justice that is more limited in scope than the story conveys
Lack of volunteer resources/time
  • All committee are short of committee members
  • Scattered focus/energy
  • Inability to let go of some activities/priorities as we add others
  • Not enough connection/reaching out among members of the congregation
